## 2. Active Theses

### 2.1 Consumer apps eat the next cycle, not infra
**Claim:** *"Pure infrastructure deals will not replicate 2021 valuation multiples. Outsized returns over the next 5 years come from consumer apps that turn crypto rails into daily Web2-grade experiences."* [Research: 消费者应用赛道_洞察与思考 (Max Wong)]

The fund's house view is that L1s/L2s/dev tools have hit diminishing marginal returns, attention is the bottleneck, and the projects with real revenue (Hyperliquid ~$900M ARR, Pump.fun ~$500M ARR, Polymarket ~$12B volume) are all consumer plays — far outperforming over-funded infra (Berachain, SEI, Story Protocol called out by name as cautionary) [Research: 消费者应用赛道]. Yiping's research piece [Research: 加密基础设施的当前状态] directly says "we are seeing too many infra platforms competing for too few attractive applications, creating ghost chains" — same conclusion.

**Citations / applied:**
- Funded under this thesis: Pump (token), Aura (consumer mobile trading), Kupid TV (Kickstarter), Rip.fun (bridge), Breezie (TCG), Dare (Kickstarter), Flip (Kickstarter, contemplated) [IC 2025-12-21 Kupid; IC 2026-02-12 Aura/Breezie; IC 2026-02-26 Rip.fun; IC 2025-12-21 Dare; IC 2026-03-26 Flip]
- Passed under this thesis (failed PMF/founder bar): Demether (tokenized stock yield), Quant.fun (hedge-fund tokenization), Callshot (skill-based betting), Datai/The Tie (B2B data API too saturated) [IC 2026-02-12, 2026-03-05, 2026-03-05, 2026-01-23]

## 5. Hard Disqualifiers

These come up repeatedly as automatic rejections (not "we'll think about it"):

1. **Anon team in custody-bearing or capital-managing protocols** — never explicit but applied in practice; every approved deal has fully-doxxed founders. The fund's underwriting process *requires* LinkedIn + reference checks (e.g. Chromatic IC asked Nick for trader-group references; ATH demanded JTCC CEO call-back; Bifrost required Lambada/Thorchain backchannel) [IC across 2026].

2. **Founder visa-status / regulatory exposure that forces pivot mid-fund-life** — Flip founders had to leave US for Australia mid-fundraise due to visa expiration; this is flagged but not a kill, but Momir explicitly said *"we'd want them based US permanently before we lead"* [IC 2026-03-26]

3. **Token economics where >50% of revenue accrues to off-chain entity with no commitment to push it back** — explicit kill on Maple/Syrup [IC 2026-04-02]. Likewise applied as concern (not kill) on Etherfi.

4. **Marketing claims unverifiable on-chain when the protocol is supposed to be transparent** — Pump's wash-volume question was specifically tested by checking pumpswap volume correlation. Pass-of-suspicion threshold: *"if we can't verify the volume, we discount it 50%"* [IC 2026-02-04]

5. **Prediction-market pricing that requires buyer or maker information asymmetry** — see Longshot Labs discussion: *"the platform discriminates against winning takers, why would they keep using it?"* [IC 2026-01-29]

6. **Founders who, upon being asked direct questions about retention/CAC/LTV in a Series-A-or-later context, can't answer** (preseed gets a pass per Joey's repeated line: *"poly market couldn't have given you these numbers in the seed stage"*) [IC 2025-12-21 Dare]

7. **"Just-Asia" framing as the only differentiation** — see Unified Labs, Buzzing.

8. **Bear-case downside that requires "the whole crypto market keeps going up"** — Treadfi discussion explicitly tested cyclicality of the farming user base [IC 2026-01-23]

## 6. Anti-Patterns

### A1 — High-FDV launches with strong-narrative-but-no-usage
The fund explicitly doesn't index on FDV/launch valuation. The repeated mantra: *"if you launched at $750M with no revenue, you're going to face supply pressure that no narrative can offset."* Genius Terminal at $750M (with CZ-backed narrative but minimal real spot volume vs. axiom) was passed primarily on this [IC 2026-03-12]. Same anti-pattern explicitly cited: Berachain, SEI, Story Protocol [Research: 消费者应用赛道].

### A2 — "We're partnering with everyone" deck slides
Bifrost/Aura/Treadfi/3F all have impressive partner slides; they get verified by backchanneling. Compute Lab claimed "Anthropic contract" — IC asked which data center, Benji had to commit to checking: *"I'd be really skeptical that compute labs has any anthropic contract directly"* (Momir) [IC 2026-04-16]. Pattern: never trust a partner slide without reference check.

### A3 — "Web3 version of [Web2 thing]" without identifying what crypto specifically unlocks
Anti-pattern. Tested directly in Demether (web3 yield on tokenized stocks), Functor (web3 password manager for agents), Veli (crypto index API). All three were passed/pushed-back-on with the question *"what does crypto specifically unlock here that you can't do with web2 alternatives?"* [IC 2026-02-12, 2026-02-12, 2026-01-29]

### A4 — Heavy operations + thin team
Rip.fun: only operational because the team has live ripping but no engineering throughput. Same trap caught Maalexi (12-team) and the various GPU-financing platforms. Pattern test: *"if they're saying their TAM is $1B, do they have the operational headcount to execute on 1% of that within 24 months?"* [IC 2026-04-02]

### A5 — "We use AI to do X better"
The fund explicitly under-prices "AI" as a differentiator. Datai/The Tie agent terminal, Insideout's AI-for-security, Rumi's vision layer — all received pushback on the basic question of whether "AI helps" actually closes a real customer gap vs. existing tooling. The bar is a structural insight that AI specifically enables (e.g. Bittensor's CHUTES showing it can offer cheaper inference than AWS only because of token subsidy — and Max's exact pushback: *"Do we even need decentralized AI?"*) [IC 2026-03-26].

### A6 — "VC validation" via cap-table without verified backchannels
Centrifuge's binance listing was a tell — IC explicitly asked *"did binance pay them to list, or did they pay binance, and what does this signal about their token-strategy intentions?"* (still TBD) [IC 2026-03-19]. Same sensitivity applied to Fun.xyz (signalfire-led was treated with skepticism: "web2 VCs underwrite differently and may exit at next markup") [IC 2026-01-15].

### A7 — Crypto-native feature added to web-2-mainstream business
Reverse anti-pattern: many deals get evaluated on whether the "crypto bit" is essential or bolted on. Veli (web2-style fund index API + token incentive) was explicitly questioned on this front; Compute Lab was reframed as "FinTech for AI financing" rather than RWA play. Pattern: *"if the crypto can be removed without breaking the core flywheel, this is a Web2 deal in disguise — invest only if web2 underwriting works."*

### A8 — "We'll figure out token later" with strong product
Treadfi explicitly chose not to launch token; Kettle is equity-only; both acquired follow-on interest. Counter-pattern: when a strong product team explicitly *does* want to launch a token, the IC's first move is to question the alignment (Etherfi case). The fund's revealed preference is **equity bets where token can be a future bonus**, not the inverse.

## 9. Vocabulary

Internal language and frameworks used by IC members. The downstream scoring agent should use this vocabulary natively.

### Roles / archetypes
- **"Cabal"** — favorable cap-table dynamic, often deployed positively (3F's prime-broker network) and negatively (Genius Terminal's CZ-cabal). The fund is sensitive to *whether* a deal is in a productive cabal or being inflated by one.
- **"World-class founder"** — used when founder has a previous successful exit + crypto-native track record + can ship under pressure. Bar set by Sonia (3F), Tim (Unifold), Mike Silagadze (Etherfi), Mika (Dare), Jordan/Joel (Flip).
- **"Hustler / scrappy" team** — alternate-bar archetype for less-pedigreed founders who have proven resilience. Kettle (4 years of pivots), Rip.fun (founders ripping cards live), Dare (Mika solo).
- **"Asia BD edge / Asia-native distribution"** — IOSG's specific value-prop to founders, not a thesis.
- **"S-tier founder" / "A-tier"** — implicit ranking; S-tier deserves Kickstarter check + reserved follow-on.
- **"Friend-of-friend cap table"** — pejorative; reused investor pools that signal demand-stuffing.

### Product / market structure
- **"PMF / pre-PMF"** — used precisely; "we're at 470 paying users + 14M API calls organic, that's PMF" (Datai); "100k email signups on a website is not PMF" (frequent pushback)
- **"Two-sided marketplace problem / chicken-and-egg"** — explicit framework. The fund tests whether one side is "already there" before betting.
- **"PvP token / zero-sum protocol"** — used negatively. Joey: *"if every dollar made by some user is lost by another, the protocol decays."*
- **"Pump pattern / Hyperliquid pattern"** — sustained revenue + token alignment + defensible network effect, the bar for liquid bets.
- **"Power-law funder bet"** — Joey's framing for Kickstarter: *"if you have 100 of these and you put $100k into each, one of them will return the fund."* [IC 2026-03-19]
- **"Stacked yield"** — Chromatic's structural insight: the only way prediction-market-style markets work for low-yield events is when the underwriter can stack yield on idle collateral. Repeatedly cited as the differentiator. [IC 2026-03-12, 2026-03-19]
- **"Adverse selection / lemon market"** — explicit framework applied to Compute Lab (founders coming on-chain because TradFi VC said no). [IC 2026-04-16]

### Token / liquid-bet language
- **"Token alignment"** vs **"buyback commitment"** — separated by Momir; alignment is about the team's intent to direct value to token; buyback is the mechanism. Different debates. [IC 2026-03-26 Etherfi]
- **"Value-accrual mechanism"** — token's contract with holders. Etherfi's "DAO governs Treasury" is one mechanism; Pump's "100% revenue → buyback" is another.
- **"Liquid universe / investable altcoins"** — Joey's formal universe of 5–10 tokens worth holding. As of Q1 2026: Hyperliquid, Pump, Etherfi (debated), Morpho, Pyth, Sky. [IC 2026-04-02 Maple]
- **"Liquid play with tripwires"** — a token bet with explicit KPI thresholds for de-risking. Etherfi gets this treatment.
- **"K-shaped recovery cycle"** — referencing Joey's framework that this cycle's winners are concentrated, unlike 2021's "everything pumps." Used to justify why narrative-driven moves matter. [Research: 对本周期山寨季的几点思考]

### Specific GTM patterns
- **"Warm pipe via [partner]"** — fund-favored deal flow signal. Unifold = MoonPay client list + Dragonfly portcos + YC pipeline.
- **"Founder-led BD / sales-led" vs "product-led"** — explicit dichotomy. Glide is product-led (lower CAC, slower scaling). Fun.xyz is sales-led (higher CAC, slower onboarding, premium pricing). Unifold is hybrid.
- **"Exclusive contract with [anchor]"** — flagged as both strength (Polymarket × Fun.xyz exclusivity) and weakness (creates concentration risk).

### Founder evaluation
- **"Doesn't dodge questions / answers directly"** — Momir's repeat frame.
- **"Stuck in their head" / "dropping vocabulary to look smart"** — pejorative, applied to John (Chromatic); the diagnosis is that the founder is using complexity to mask weak GTM. [IC 2026-03-19]
- **"Doer / hustler"** — positive (Soar founders traveling to meet protocols in person).
- **"SBF-style intensity"** — Joey on Simcluster's Harvey Pratt; *"high conviction, opinionated, contrarian, eccentric"* — flagged as a risk-management point but not necessarily a kill. [IC 2026-03-19]

## 10. Evolution Notes

### E1 — Pre-Q3 2025: heavy infra bias → Q4 2025+: explicit consumer/payment shift

The fund's stated thesis explicitly evolved during the corpus period. Yiping's research piece [Research: 加密基础设施的当前状态] and Max's [Research: 消费者应用赛道] both written in Q3–Q4 2025 mark the formal pivot. In meetings, this manifests as the partners explicitly downweighting infra deals: *"basically every infra is just competing for the same five dApps that work"* [Research: 加密基础设施的当前状态]. By Q1 2026 the fund is consciously avoiding new L1/L2/dev-tool bets and concentrating on consumer apps + crypto payments.

### E2 — Pre-Q3 2025: enthusiastic about prediction markets → Q4 2025+: skeptical except where structurally novel

Compare Mario's [Research: 透过_Kalshi_探究预测市场] (October-ish, fairly bullish) with Jiawei's [Research: 给预测市场浇盆冷水] (November-ish, explicit cold water). The IC has moved decisively to Jiawei's framing. Buzzing, XO, Limitless-clones are now reflexively passed unless they offer a structural primitive (Longshot's parlays, Chromatic's stacked yield).

### E3 — Pre-Q4 2025: gaming pessimism → still selective but slight thaw

Joey's [Research: 加密游戏从泡沫到自增长真实玩家之路解析] (early-cycle) was bearish on Web3 gaming. By 2026 IC discussions, gaming is re-entered tactically — Breezie/Rip.fun/ATH all received serious consideration as TCG-collectibles plays (which is gaming-adjacent). Sid's [Research: 游戏_AI_代理和加密货币的融合] explicitly says "the bubble has popped, capital flowing to higher-quality games." Active deals: Breezie (followed not invested), Rip.fun (small bridge round).

### E4 — Pre-Q1 2026: token-launch enthusiasm → Q2 2026+: equity-first preference

Multiple deals show this: Treadfi explicitly chose not to launch token; Kettle is equity-only; Centrifuge debating token-to-equity conversion; Dare explicitly rejected previous token-launch playbook. Frame: *"token can be a future bonus on equity, not the inverse."* This is a real shift from 2024-era IOSG which led many token rounds. Drivers: 2024 token underperformance, "high-FDV-low-float" critique [Research: 对本周期山寨季的几点思考].

### E5 — Pre-Q4 2025: agentic AI excitement → Q1 2026: skepticism

[Research: 借助_AI_交易_初探_DeFai_生态] (early-2025 enthusiastic) and [Research: 机器的经济秩序_智能体商业的全栈路径] (late 2025 — measured) frame this. By 2026 IC discussions, agentic AI is treated as narrative-only unless the project has real customers (Spore.fun, Bittensor's CHUTES). Functor passed; Rumi vibe-pass; Insideout passed. Survivors: tracked TAO at small size with halving-as-tripwire.

### E6 — Stablecoin payments: from "interesting infra" → "the entire next 3 years"

Frank's [Research: 再造十个空中云汇_稳定币加密支付赛道前景] and Sam's [Research: 一文读懂稳定币公链] are the canonical statements of where the fund is heading. By Q1 2026 IC time, payments deals (Glide, Unifold, Immersve, Kettle, Fun.xyz tracked) make up roughly 30% of attention. Kettle's evolution — from NFT marketplace → core payment platform — is the model.

### E7 — Hyperliquid: from "another perp DEX" → "platform-level bet"

Mario's early [Research: 新的_Degen_擂台_Hyperliquid] established the basics; Joey's [Research: Hyperliquid_指南] solidified the platform thesis (HyperEVM + builder codes + mobile DeFi). By Q1 2026 the fund is making secondary bets on the HL ecosystem — Aura (perps.fun), Bifrost (cross-margin on HyperEVM), Treadfi (market-making bots), even prediction-market integrations.

### E8 — Anti-infra-but-pro-stablecoin-chains

The fund's revealed pattern: rejecting generic L1/L2 deals but embracing stablecoin-native chains (Plasma, Stable, Arc, Tempo, Codex per Frank's research) as a *different category*. Sam's [Research: 一文读懂稳定币公链] formalizes the thesis: *"stablecoin payment chains have achieved necessary scale and stability — the next phase is consumer-grade UX, programmable compliance, gasless transactions."* This is one of the few infra theses the fund is actively pursuing.

### E9 — Pre-Q1 2026: skeptical of "Asian distribution" arguments → Q2 2026: explicit reframing

The IC has become more discriminating about geographic-edge claims. Unified Labs ("Eastern Steakhouse") was rejected *for the geographic argument*; conversely 3F's "Asian institutional capital" was treated as a valuation driver but not as the core thesis. The shift: geography is a *boost* on top of an otherwise-validated thesis, never the standalone thesis. Buzzing was passed for this reason despite Danny's strong founder advocacy.

### E10 — Increasing rigor on revenue verification

Pump is the cleanest example: original summary trusted the volume numbers, IC follow-up explicitly demanded backchanneling and 50% discount on volume figures. Compute Lab: founder claims "$5B pipeline" → IC pushed for client names. Maple: founder reports gross revenue → IC explicitly traced where 75% goes. By Q1 2026, the standard practice is to ask "how would you verify this number on-chain or via backchannel?" before approving.
