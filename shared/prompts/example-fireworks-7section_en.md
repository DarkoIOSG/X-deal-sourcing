Compact version. Use when a deal-flow doc needs to pack in 10+ projects and readers ask to "read one within a single screen." Fold `risks & upsides` into `differentiation`, drop the `conclusion` section, and give the direction in one sentence at the end.

## [Fireworks AI](https://fireworks.ai) 🟢

- **One-liner intro:** AI inference cloud — runs open-source LLMs as production-grade APIs for enterprises, compressing "the inference infrastructure that took Meta 5 years to build internally" into a 5-week delivery. Two product lines: **FireAttention/FireOptimizer** (proprietary CUDA kernels + speculative decoding) + **full-stack fine-tuning + Eval Protocol** (SFT/DPO/RFT + deployment + evaluation in one place).
- **Value proposition:** Solves the most expensive problem of the AI-application era — **inference cost and latency are eating up 80% of the gross margin of Agent / Copilot apps.** Claims to be 12x faster than vLLM and 40x faster than the GPT-4 baseline, can attach 100+ LoRA adapters on a single GPU, and lets customers serve 3-5x more customers on the same H200 during GPU-shortage cycles. Sits in the "selling shovels to every company running AI apps" position.
- **Target customer:** AI-native high-growth apps (Cursor, Perplexity, Genspark, Retell AI) + traditional large enterprises going AI (Notion, Uber, DoorDash, Shopify, Samsung, Upwork, GitLab, Sourcegraph, Quora). 10,000+ enterprise customers, jumping from ~1,000 to 10,000+ over the past year.
- **Alternatives:** **Together AI** (academic lineage / the team of FlashAttention author Tri Dao, ARR $1B+, this round in talks at $7.5B pre), **Baseten** (enterprise-grade inference + Observability, Jan 2026 Series E $300M @ $5B), **Modal** (developer serverless full-stack, ARR ~$50M, Series B $87M @ $1.1B), **Hyperscalers** (AWS Bedrock / Azure AI Foundry / Google Vertex — strong channels but slow performance and customer lock-in); customers' DIY alternative is running their own vLLM + H100 cluster — but that requires a PyTorch-level engineering team to make work.
- **Sector capitalization status:** AI inference cloud is the most capital-concentrated infra subsegment of 2025-26. **Fireworks** is in Series D talks at $10B post (co-led by Benchmark + DST; the other firm that gave a term sheet offered $15B post), $327M raised cumulatively; **Together** $534M cumulative + this round at $1B @ $8.5B post in talks; **Baseten** $585M cumulative, Series E @ $5B; **Modal** $222M cumulative, rumored $2.5B new round in talks. Sacra estimates the inference market at $100B+ within 5 years.
- **The VC thesis:** AI is moving from the "model era" into the "inference era" — whoever masters "running more tokens out of the same GPU" masters the application-layer gross-margin curve of the Agent/Copilot era, analogous to AWS relative to the Web 2.0 SaaS era — abstracting away complex infra so the application layer can focus on building product. OpenRouter data shows inference demand grew 4x over the past 12 months, projected to support $10B in revenue within 3 years (AWS 2026 ARR ~$150B).
- **Differentiation:** **The PyTorch founding team** — CEO Lin Qiao led PyTorch at Meta for 7 years (300+ engineers), and the co-founders are all core maintainers of PyTorch / Meta infra / Google Vertex AI. This means kernel-level optimization IP (FireAttention V4 / speculative decoding / multi-LoRA) is a moat that Together (academic lineage, engineering depth yet to be proven), Baseten (mostly application-layer, weak hardware co-design), and Modal (mostly serverless infra) will struggle to match for 3-5 years. 30-50% higher throughput at the same compute directly equals a gross-margin lead. **The only independent inference cloud simultaneously embraced by NVIDIA + AMD + Databricks + MongoDB + Microsoft Foundry.** **Risks:** (1) the valuation jumped from $4B to $10B (2.5x) in 7 months — if ARR growth drops from 4x to 2x, there's significant mark-down risk in the next round; (2) Hyperscaler counterattack — AWS Bedrock / Azure / Vertex are all increasing investment in inference optimization and may eventually use channel advantages to squeeze out independent players; (3) closed-source model reflux — if GPT-5 / Claude 5 once again open up a quality gap, enterprises may return to closed-source APIs. **Upsides:** (a) 141 people supporting $300M+ ARR (~$2M ARR per employee) is rare productivity for an infra company; (b) Microsoft Foundry chose Fireworks over building its own — a closed-source cloud giant endorsing it itself = free channel leverage; (c) the PyTorch founding team = recruiting flywheel + priority cooperation with the open-source ecosystem (Llama / DeepSeek / Kimi / Qwen all treat Fireworks as a Day-0 launch partner).
