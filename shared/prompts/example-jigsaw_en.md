## [Jigsaw](https://jigsaw.build/?utm_source=luma)

- **One-liner intro:** Applied research lab — automatically generates "simulated environments" for frontier AI labs and enterprises, and trains world-generation foundation models. Two product lines: **Darwin** (world-generation foundation model) + a **scalable RL-environment platform** (lets domain experts contribute intelligence to models without writing code).
- **Value proposition:** Solves the biggest bottleneck of the RL post-training era — **a severe scarcity of high-quality, verifiable training environments.** It automates the "domain expert → model capability" pipeline (no engineer in the loop), while using a proprietary world model to generate high-fidelity simulated environments. Sits in the "selling shovels to frontier labs" position.
- **Target customer:** Frontier AI labs (OpenAI / Anthropic / Google DeepMind / xAI / Meta), mid-to-large enterprises building agents (which need vertical RL environments), robotics/autonomous-driving companies (which need world simulation), and defense/research institutions.
- **Alternatives:** **Mercor** (expert-data marketplace, valuation $2B+), **Scale AI** (Meta took a 49% stake at $14B), **Surge AI** (already profitable, annual revenue $1B+), **Turing** (backed by Vista, valuation $5B) — these mainly do "human-labeled data"; **World Labs** (Fei-Fei Li, valuation $1B+, raised $230M) does spatial intelligence / world models; **Decart** (raised $32M) does real-time world models; **Genie 3** (Google DeepMind, closed-source); **Prime Intellect** (decentralized RL training); **General Reasoning** (YC, RL environments).
- **Sector capitalization status:** **RL Environments + World Models is the most capital-dense subsegment of 2025–26** — both OpenAI and Anthropic have publicly stated that the post-training bottleneck has shifted from "compute" to "environment quality." **Mercor** jumped from a $250M valuation to $2B+ within a year, with nearly all the money coming from frontier labs buying expert data; **World Labs** is valued at $1B+; **Decart** raised $32M (Sequoia + Oren Etzioni); **Decagon, Mechanize, Prime Intellect** each raised tens of millions of dollars in RL environments / post-training infra.
- **The VC thesis:** In the post-training era, whoever controls "environment generation" controls the model-capability curve — analogous to Scale AI relative to supervised learning in 2018.
- **Differentiation:** **Simultaneously doing a world-model foundation model (Darwin) + a platform layer (productized RL environments) + letting experts contribute directly (bypassing engineers)** — a full-stack play. Risks: (1) this sector has extremely concentrated customers (only a handful of frontier labs), so single-vendor risk is high; (2) Scale/Mercor are already the default vendors, so a new entrant must have proprietary technology (Darwin has to genuinely work); (3) frontier labs are also internalizing this themselves (both OpenAI and Anthropic are building in-house RL-environment teams). Upsides: the founders/team are research-native, and the product naming (Darwin) and narrative ("world generation") sit squarely on the consensus direction of the AI community.
